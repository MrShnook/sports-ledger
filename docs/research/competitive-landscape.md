# Competitive Landscape — SportsLedger

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Researched by:** Mr. Shnook (ventures-self-drive cron)

---

## Executive Summary

**There is no dedicated youth sports family expense tracker in existence.** The closest competitors fall into two categories: (1) team management platforms that handle payments from the organization side, and (2) generic personal finance apps that lack sport-specific categorization. This confirms the empty niche identified in our scout brief.

---

## Category 1: Team Management Platforms

These serve coaches/admins — NOT families tracking their total spend.

### TeamSnap
- **Pricing:** Free for parents. Team admins pay $0 (free, up to 15 members), $9.99-15.99/mo (Premium, 40 members), $12.50-21.99/mo (Ultra, unlimited). No parent-facing subscription.
- **Family Features:** View schedules, rosters, chat, game updates, track team payments/fees. No expense tracking, budgeting, or cost analysis from the family side.
- **Key Limitation:** Parents see *what they owe* to the team, not *what they've spent total* across tournaments, travel, equipment, multiple teams, etc. TeamSnap is about team logistics, not family finances.
- **No "TeamSnap for Families" product** — there's no standalone family expense feature. Integration is through the team.
- **Gap we fill:** Total cost visibility. A parent with 2 kids on 3 teams uses TeamSnap to see each team's fees but has NO aggregated view of total youth sports spending.

### SportsEngine
- **Pricing:** $58-69/mo for organizations (billed annually). Free for parents.
- **Features:** Registration, scheduling, payment processing, financial tracking (org-side).
- **Key Limitation:** Same as TeamSnap — organization-facing tool. Parents interact through their team's portal. No family expense tracking.
- **Often provided free** via sport governing bodies (US Soccer, USA Hockey, etc.)

### LeagueApps
- **Pricing:** Not publicly listed; subscription for organizations.
- **Features:** Payment plans, automated reminders, financial reports — all for the org.
- **Key Limitation:** Zero family-facing expense features.

### Jersey Watch
- **Pricing:** Not publicly listed.
- **Features:** Fee tracking per player, payment plans, automated receipts — for team admins.

### SportsPlus
- **Pricing:** Subscription-based for organizations.
- **Features:** Automated payments, custom invoices, QuickBooks sync.
- **Key Limitation:** Org-side accounting, not family-side budgeting.

### Onsides (NEW — ~2024 beta)
- **What it is:** Parent-focused app that aggregates schedules across platforms (TeamSnap, SportsEngine, etc.)
- **Features:** Logistics/scheduling AI, calendar aggregation. NO expense tracking.
- **Worth watching:** If they add expense features, they'd be a direct competitor. But as of 2026, they're logistics-only.

## Category 2: General Personal Finance Apps

### YNAB (You Need A Budget)
- **Pricing:** $14.99/mo ($99/yr)
- **Approach:** Envelope budgeting — users create their own categories
- **Youth sports capability:** You CAN create manual categories for "Soccer Fees," "Travel," "Equipment." But...
  - No pre-built sport categories
  - No multi-child tracking (one budget for the whole family)
  - No receipt scanning with auto-categorization
  - No sport benchmarks
  - No season forecasting
  - No tax-relevant flagging
  - Overkill for families who only want to track sports costs (full budgeting commitment)
- **Key insight:** YNAB is a general budgeting tool. Parents who use it for sports expenses are essentially building a custom spreadsheet inside YNAB. We're purpose-built.

### Mint / Credit Karma (now merged)
- **Pricing:** Free
- **Auto-categorization:** Lumps sports expenses under "Entertainment," "Shopping," or "Education." No youth sports category exists.
- **Key Limitation:** Can't distinguish between "Amazon purchase of soccer cleats" and "Amazon purchase of dog food." No subcategory granularity for sports. Mint was sunset/absorbed into Credit Karma in 2024 — less reliable now.

### Rocket Money (formerly Truebill)
- **Pricing:** Free basic; Premium $6-12/mo
- **Features:** Subscription tracking, bill negotiation, spending categorization.
- **Key Limitation:** Similar to Mint — no sport-specific categories. Good at finding recurring charges but useless for one-off tournament fees, gas, hotels.

### Copilot Money
- **Pricing:** $10.99/mo
- **Features:** Smart categorization, clean UI, joint account support.
- **Key Limitation:** Still generic categories. No youth sports awareness.

### Splitwise / Venmo
- **Pricing:** Free (Splitwise Pro $4.99/mo)
- **Usage:** Parents use Splitwise to split carpool gas, hotel rooms at tournaments, team dinner costs.
- **Key Limitation:** Splitting costs ≠ tracking total spend. These are reimbursement tools, not budgeting tools.

## Category 3: DIY Solutions

### Spreadsheet Templates
- **No widely shared youth sports expense spreadsheet templates found** in app stores, template marketplaces, or parent communities.
- Parents build custom Google Sheets/Excel files from scratch.
- Common approach: tabs per child or per season, manual categorization.
- Pain: time-consuming to set up, no mobile-friendly entry, no auto-categorization.

### FlipGive
- **What it is:** Cashback platform for sports teams. Parents shop through FlipGive, earn cashback that goes to the team fund.
- **Not a competitor:** This is fundraising, not expense tracking. But it's in the same ecosystem — parents who use FlipGive are exactly our target users.
- **Partnership opportunity:** We could integrate FlipGive cashback data to show "you spent $12K on soccer but earned $400 back through FlipGive."

## Key Takeaways

1. **The niche is genuinely empty.** Not "we think it's empty" — research confirms NO product exists.
2. **Team platforms serve organizations, not families.** TeamSnap/SportsEngine help teams collect money. They don't help families understand what they're spending.
3. **Generic finance apps lack granularity.** YNAB can be forced to work, but it's like using a bulldozer to plant flowers.
4. **The True Cost Calculator has no equivalent anywhere.** This is our most differentiated feature — showing the REAL cost of committing to a team before signing up.
5. **FlipGive is a potential partner**, not a competitor. Same audience, complementary features.
6. **Onsides is the one to watch.** They're building a parent-first sports app — if they add expense tracking, they'd be closest to us. But they're logistics-focused today.
